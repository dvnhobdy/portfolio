The Focus

I pinpointed two potential problem areas that would drive the redesign:

The first problem area was the navigational experience. At the time, users landed in the middle of a post, and scrolled through a sea of content in the hopes of finding something interesting. How could I make this experience less tedious/feel more tailored to discerning readers?

The second area was content organization. At the time, content wasn't grouped, making it a chore to read related posts unless they were posted in succession. What would the most effective ways of categorizing + surfacing related content look like?

The Discovery Process

The Design Process

I began sketching out different user flows. The most obvious experience begins at the content hub + guides users through their catgeory of choice, to the most relevant (set of) articles.

But after consulting our social media team, I realized that people most often navigate straight to an article from a twitter or instagram link, so it was important to make sure that these kinds of users could easily click through relevant content without having to return to the main page.

Our Solution

With these flows mapped out I got to work on the specifics of content categorization. I opted for a tabbed solution on the homepage that encouraged users to narrow their reading experience. They would see relevant featured articles, followed by other articles of their chosen type.

Then I adopted a tagging solution in articles, that directed users to a page of similarly tagged articles. I also added a similar post link at the bottom of the page that would use tags to surface related articles.

We moved forward with archiving older articles. Because of the existing Wordpress framework, my engineering partner was able to roll older posts into the newer design. He scraped those posts for relevant keywords, and was able to retroactively assign tags to these pages, making them more searchable for newer readers, and connecting them to newer articles for older ones.

The UI was driven by the existing design language of soundcloud.com, combining the readability of interstate with the high contrast orange, dark grey, + white palette SoundCloud is known for.
